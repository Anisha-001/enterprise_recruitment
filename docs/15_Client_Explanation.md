# Client Project Guide: How the Recruitment System Works

This guide explains the recruitment system in plain English. No programming knowledge is required to understand this document.

---

## 1. What is this System?

Think of this software as a **Digital Hiring Assistant**. It replaces spreadsheets, endless email threads, and paper forms with a single online tool. It helps your team advertise job openings, review applicant resumes, schedule interviews, grade candidates, and send digital job offers.

It has three main parts:
1. **The Careers Website**: The public-facing job board where job seekers browse open positions and submit their applications.
2. **The Candidate Portal**: A private dashboard where applicants can view their application status, see scheduled interview dates, upload documents, and sign job offers.
3. **The Admin Control Room**: An internal dashboard for your recruiters and hiring managers to review candidates, coordinate interviews, and draft offer packages.

---

## 2. Step-by-Step Hiring Journey

Here is how a candidate moves through your system:

### Step 1: Browsing & Applying
A job seeker visits your careers page, filters job openings by location or department, and submits their application. They upload their resume and answer screening questions (e.g., "Do you require visa sponsorship?").

### Step 2: Welcome & Portal Access
Once submitted, the system automatically checks if this candidate has applied before (preventing duplicate profiles). It then sends the candidate a welcome email with a secure link to create a password and access their private portal dashboard.

### Step 3: Screening & Review
Your HR team sees the new application in the admin panel. They inspect the candidate's experience, grade them (1 to 5 stars), and drag-and-drop their card to move them from **New Application** to **Screening**. The candidate is updated automatically via email.

### Step 4: Scheduling Interviews
Recruiters schedule interview stages (e.g., Technical Round or Manager Round) directly in the system. They select a date, time, and video platform (like Zoom or Google Meet). The system emails calendar invites to the candidate and your interviewers.

### Step 5: Scoring Candidate Performance
After the interview, your managers fill out a simple scorecard rating the candidate and listing pros, cons, and recommendations. This scorecard is saved directly to the candidate's profile for review.

### Step 6: Extending & Signing the Job Offer
When you find the right candidate, you draft a job offer directly in the tool, breaking down basic salary, housing, and transport allowances. The system generates an offer letter PDF and emails it to the candidate.
The candidate logs into their portal, reviews the terms, types their name in the signature box, and clicks **Accept**. The system records their digital signature and client IP address for compliance.

### Step 7: Hired!
Once signed, your team marks them as **Hired** in the system. The software automatically updates their status, notifies the team, and sets up a new employee record.
